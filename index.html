💡 WHAT IS A FACT?
📌 Definition of a Fact
A fact is a statement that can be objectively verified and proven to be true through evidence, research, or direct observation. Facts are not opinions, guesses, or beliefs — they are supported by credible sources, data, or scientific consensus.
✅ Example of a Fact "The Earth orbits the Sun once every 365.25 days." — This is verifiable through astronomy and scientific measurement.
❌ Not a Fact (Opinion) "The Sun is the most beautiful thing in the universe." — This cannot be objectively proven.
🛡️ How to Stay Away from Rumors
01
Verify the Source
Always check if the news comes from a credible, established outlet. Anonymous or unknown sources are a red flag.
02
Cross-Check with Multiple Sources
If only one source reports it, be skeptical. Genuine news is usually covered by several independent outlets.
03
Watch for Emotional Language
Rumors often use shocking, exaggerated, or highly emotional words to trigger a reaction before you think critically.
04
Check the Date
Old news is often reshared out of context to mislead. Always check when an article was originally published.
05
Use Fact-Checking Tools
Use platforms like Snopes, FactCheck.org, or this very tool — TruthLens — to verify claims before sharing.
🛡️ SAFETY FIRST
⚠️
Misinformation spreads faster than facts. Every share matters — be the shield, not the spreader.
🔍 Types of Misinformation
MISINFORMATION False content shared without intent to harm — the person believes it's true.
DISINFORMATION Deliberately fabricated or manipulated content created with intent to deceive.
MALINFORMATION True information shared with malicious intent to harm a person or group.
DEEPFAKES AI-generated fake videos or audio designed to make real people appear to say or do things they never did.
🔐 How to Protect Yourself
01
Pause Before You Share
Take 30 seconds to ask — "Do I know this is true?" Sharing false news makes you part of the problem, even unintentionally.
02
Verify Images and Videos
Use Google Reverse Image Search or tools like InVID to check if images/videos are real or taken out of context.
03
Be Skeptical of Viral Content
Content that goes viral very quickly is often emotionally charged. The more shocking it is, the more you should verify it first.
04
Protect Your Personal Data
Misinformation campaigns often harvest user data. Avoid clicking unverified links, especially in news-style WhatsApp forwards.
05
Report Fake Content
Use the "Report" feature on social media platforms to flag misinformation. Reporting helps remove harmful content faster.
06
Educate Those Around You
If a friend or family member shares false news, politely correct them with the real facts and a reliable source. Community safety starts with you.
